Donington:
The Final Fight

A strong way to close the season, with race pace, fightbacks and proof of progress under pressure.

Race Report
15 Oct 2025 · 1 min read

The final round of the season took us to Donington Park. Practice went well, sitting comfortably inside the top ten and feeling confident with the car.

Qualifying was tricky. The track was drying, conditions kept changing and a few mistakes cost us. We ended up P17 for Race 1 and P15 for Race 2. Not ideal, but the race pace was there.

Race 1 was one of the strongest drives of the year in terms of racecraft and consistency. It was hard, clean racing all the way through, fighting forward from P17 to P10.

Race 2 started well and we climbed to P7, but another driver missed the braking point and took me out completely. The contact caused heavy damage and put the final race in doubt.

Race 3 was impossible to start because of the damage from Race 2. It was not the way we wanted to end the weekend, but that is racing.

Overall, it was still a strong weekend and a positive way to close the season. We showed how much we evolved mentally, technically and as a team. I am proud of the progress and ready for what comes next.
